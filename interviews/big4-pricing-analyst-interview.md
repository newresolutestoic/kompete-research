## 🆕 New Insights (We Didn't Know This)

### 1. Survey Data is Critical (Not Just Competitive Analysis)
**What Rashi Said:**
- **100-300 respondent surveys** with conjoint analysis
- Measures customer willingness to pay
- Tests price increase scenarios (20-30%)
- Churn modeling based on price sensitivity

**Product Implication:**
- Phase 1 (MVP): Competitive benchmarking ✅ (what we have)
- Phase 2: Add survey capabilities or integrate with survey tools
- Phase 3: Willingness-to-pay calculator based on market data

**Not MVP, but future roadmap item**

---

### 2. 5-10 Competitors is Standard Benchmark Size
**What Rashi Said:**
- Typical analysis: 5-10 competitors
- Distinguish direct vs indirect
- Track market leader separately

**Product Implication:**
- Our "track up to 10 competitors" is PERFECT
- Don't need unlimited - 5-10 is industry standard
- Freemium: 3 competitors
- Paid: 10 competitors
- Enterprise: Unlimited

---

### 3. Feature Presence/Absence Tracking
**What Rashi Said:**
> "Detailed feature lists across competitors, marking presence or absence. Number of competitors offering a feature indicates essentiality."

**Current KPMG Process:**
- Manually compile feature lists
- Check each competitor's website
- Validate exact wording
- Note unique offerings

**Product Implication:**
- Our feature gap analysis (Gap/Unique/Over-gated badges) is EXACTLY this
- We already built what they manually do!
- Opportunity: Add "% of competitors offering this feature"

---

### 4. Market Feedback Channels Matter
**What Rashi Said:**
> "G2, Product Hunt, Reddit, Twitter for customer sentiment and feature reception"

**Product Implication:**
- Phase 2 feature: Sentiment analysis from reviews
- Scrape G2/Capterra reviews for competitor products
- Track Product Hunt launches
- Monitor Reddit/Twitter mentions
- Show "market perception" not just pricing

---

### 5. Descriptive Not Prescriptive
**What Rashi Said:**
> "The product would deliver diagnostic and descriptive insights, not prescriptive pricing recommendations. Support informed decision-making."

**CRITICAL INSIGHT:**
- Don't try to tell customers "charge $X"
- Instead: "Here's what competitors charge, here's the gaps"
- Leave final decision to humans
- Consultants and analysts use our data to make recommendations

**Product Implication:**
- Change messaging from "We tell you what to charge"
- To: "We show you the competitive landscape, you decide"
- Scorecard shows gaps, not "you should fix this"
- More diagnostic, less prescriptive

---

### 6. Repricing Cycles and Triggers
**What Rashi Said:**
- Average repricing: **Every 3 years**
- Triggers: New features (AI), market shifts, PE pressure
- Price increases: **12-30%** over 4 years tested

**Product Implication:**
- Customers need continuous monitoring (not one-time audit)
- Alert when competitors add major features (AI, SSO, etc.)
- Track competitor price changes over time
- Help customers time their repricing
