# Persona: CRO / VP Sales / Head of Sales

**Last updated:** 2026-03-03
**Status:** Hypothesis — needs validation through interviews

---

## Profile

- **Title:** CRO, Chief Sales Officer, VP Sales, Head of Revenue
- **Company stage:** Series B+ ($10M-$500M ARR)
- **Company type:** B2B SaaS, enterprise software
- **Reports to:** CEO
- **Direct reports:** AEs, SDRs/BDRs, Sales Engineering, Sales Ops
- **Budget authority:** Controls sales tools budget, influences marketing tool spend
- **Time spent on competitive intel today:** Consumes outputs (battle cards, decks). Rarely creates. 30 min-1 hr/week.

## What They Care About
- Win rates, especially on competitive deals
- AE ramp time (new hires need competitive context fast)
- Deal velocity (competitive intel that accelerates or unblocks deals)
- Revenue retention (losing customers to competitors)
- Real-time intel during active deals (what is the competitor quoting?)
- Pricing competitiveness

## Hypothesis Pain Points

### HP-CRO1: AEs Go Into Competitive Deals Blind
**Hypothesis:** AEs frequently encounter competitors in deals without adequate preparation. Battle cards exist but are outdated, generic, or not consumed. AEs rely on tribal knowledge and "ask around" Slack channels.
- **Evidence from interviews:** Tanvir (HackerRank) — hires external agencies to pose as buyers for competitor demos. Soubhik (DemandBase) — "For sales, the intelligence is a gold mine of data."
- **Testable question:** "When an AE discovers a competitor in a deal, what do they do? Where do they go for competitive info? How often is that info current and useful?"

### HP-CRO2: No Real-Time Competitive Intel During Live Deals
**Hypothesis:** During active sales cycles (3-9 months for enterprise), the competitive landscape changes but the sales team doesn't get real-time updates. A competitor might change pricing, launch a feature, or win a reference customer mid-deal, and the AE doesn't know.
- **Evidence from interviews:** Lakshay (Genpact) — "competitor gave the same product at 25% less behind their back to the same client." Siddharth Jain (LeadSquared) — "Pulse daily sales team... before it will come in public domain."
- **Testable question:** "Has a deal ever been lost because a competitor changed something (pricing, feature, reference) mid-cycle that your AE didn't know about? How quickly does competitive information reach your AEs during active deals?"
- **Validation status:** [ ] Not tested  [ ] Partially validated  [ ] Validated  [ ] Invalidated

### HP-CRO3: Win/Loss Data is Unreliable
**Hypothesis:** CROs don't trust CRM data on competitive win/loss reasons. AEs fill out "competitor" and "loss reason" fields inconsistently. There's no reliable way to identify patterns in why deals are won or lost to specific competitors.
- **Evidence from interviews:** Sankalp (HighRadius) — "We never knew the compete scenario... that was a big flag." Anjali (Anaplan) — tries to average out competitor pricing from deal-level win/loss metrics which "is not absolutely accurate."
- **Testable question:** "How confident are you in your competitive win/loss data? What percentage of closed-lost deals have an accurate competitor and loss reason? Can you identify the top 3 reasons you lose to Competitor X?"
- **Validation status:** [ ] Not tested  [ ] Partially validated  [ ] Validated  [ ] Invalidated

### HP-CRO4: New AE Ramp on Competitive Landscape is Too Slow
**Hypothesis:** When new AEs join, it takes 2-4 weeks for them to understand the competitive landscape well enough to handle objections confidently. There's no structured competitive onboarding — it's "shadow calls and ask your manager."
- **Evidence from interviews:** Sankalp (HighRadius) — competitive onboarding for new employees is a use case. Tanvir — evaluated UpLimit ($10-12K/yr) for pitch practice.
- **Testable question:** "How do new AEs learn about competitors? How long before they can confidently handle competitive objections? What does your competitive onboarding look like?"
- **Validation status:** [ ] Not tested  [ ] Partially validated  [ ] Validated  [ ] Invalidated

### HP-CRO5: Can't Quantify Revenue Lost to Competitors
**Hypothesis:** CROs cannot accurately quantify how much revenue is being lost to specific competitors or identify which competitor is the biggest revenue threat. They have anecdotal data but not systematic intelligence.
- **Evidence from interviews:** Siddharth Jain (LeadSquared) — "If it builds over time it gives me how many customers I have lost to competition. That's a business value." This was explicitly flagged as what would make CI worth paying for.
- **Testable question:** "Can you tell me how much revenue you lost to Competitor X last quarter? Which competitor is your biggest revenue threat? How do you track this?"
- **Validation status:** [ ] Not tested  [ ] Partially validated  [ ] Validated  [ ] Invalidated

### HP-CRO6: Competitive Pricing Intelligence is Guesswork
**Hypothesis:** AEs go into pricing negotiations without knowing what competitors are charging. They rely on anecdotal data from past deals and gut-feel discounting, often leaving money on the table or losing deals on price unnecessarily.
- **Evidence from interviews:** Anjali (Anaplan) — relies on hiring ex-competitor employees for pricing intel. Lakshay (Genpact) — Everest Group benchmarks cost millions and are still inaccurate. Shobit — "We don't even put too much effort looking at competition pricing after the first six months."
- **Testable question:** "When an AE is in a pricing negotiation, what competitor pricing data do they have? How do they decide how much to discount? Have you ever lost a deal where you could have won by adjusting price if you'd known what the competitor quoted?"
- **Validation status:** [ ] Not tested  [ ] Partially validated  [ ] Validated  [ ] Invalidated

### HP-CRO7: Customer Churn to Competitors is a Surprise
**Hypothesis:** When existing customers churn to a competitor, the CRO finds out after the fact. There's no early warning system for customers evaluating competitors.
- **Evidence from interviews:** Siddharth Jain — "If your data can tell them, hey, this customer which is your target domain has been acquired by a competitor. That's a business value."
- **Testable question:** "When was the last time a customer left for a competitor? How did you find out? Was there any warning? What would an early warning system look like for you?"

## Buying Triggers
- Lost a major deal to a competitor
- Win rate drops below target
- New competitor enters and starts winning deals
- Sales team complains about lack of competitive material
- QBR reveals customer churn to specific competitor
- New AE cohort needs onboarding
- Board asks "why are we losing to X?"

## How They'd Measure ROI
- Win rate improvement on competitive deals (even 2-3% = massive at scale)
- Revenue saved from churn prevention
- AE ramp time reduction (weeks → days)
- Deal velocity on competitive deals
- Revenue attributed to competitive intelligence

## Likely Objections
- "My sales team already knows the market — they talk to customers every day."
- "We have battle cards from marketing. They're fine."
- "I need this in Salesforce, not another tool."
- "Show me a direct revenue correlation before I pay."
- "My AEs won't use another tool."
