# Persona: CEO / Founder

**Last updated:** 2026-03-03
**Status:** Hypothesis — needs validation through interviews

---

## Profile

- **Title:** CEO, Co-Founder, Managing Director
- **Company stage:** Series A to Series D ($1M-$100M ARR)
- **Company type:** B2B SaaS, enterprise software, tech-enabled services
- **Reports to:** Board of Directors, Investors
- **Direct reports:** CXO team, VP-level leaders
- **Time spent on competitive intel today:** 2-5 hrs/week (ad hoc, LinkedIn scrolling, board prep)

## What They Care About
- Market positioning and differentiation narrative
- Board meeting readiness (competitive landscape slides)
- Investor confidence (showing awareness of competitive threats)
- Strategic pricing decisions that impact revenue trajectory
- Identifying M&A targets or partnership opportunities
- Not getting blindsided by competitor moves

---

## Hypothesis Pain Points

### HP-F1: Board & Investor Blind Spots
**Hypothesis:** Founders spend 3-5 hours before every board meeting manually assembling competitive landscape slides, and still worry they're missing something that a board member will ask about.
- **Evidence from interviews:** Siddharth Jain (LeadSquared) — leadership relies on sales team pulse, not systematic tracking. Rohan (Inkle) — CEO personally monitors LinkedIn daily.
- **Testable question:** "How do you prepare the competitive section for your board deck? How long does it take? Have you ever been surprised by a board member bringing up a competitor you weren't tracking?"

### HP-F2: Pricing Decisions Are Gut-Feel
**Hypothesis:** Founders make pricing decisions based on intuition, conversations with advisors, and incomplete competitor data rather than systematic competitive pricing intelligence.
- **Evidence from interviews:** Shobit (Locubator) — "We don't keep changing the price and don't even put much effort looking at competition pricing after the first six months." Lakshay (Genpact) — competitor pricing is opaque, even for enterprises.
- **Testable question:** "Walk me through your last pricing decision. What competitor pricing data did you have? What did you wish you had?"
- **Validation status:** [ ] Not tested  [ ] Partially validated  [ ] Validated  [ ] Invalidated

### HP-F3: Competitor Moves Discovered Too Late
**Hypothesis:** Founders learn about significant competitor moves (fundraise, product launch, major customer win, pricing change) weeks or months after they happen, forcing reactive rather than proactive strategy.
- **Evidence from interviews:** Tanvir (HackerRank) — competitor launched same product 2 days before them, didn't know until launch day. Hareesh — "the unknown unknown... we don't know that competition is being built."
- **Testable question:** "Tell me about the last time a competitor did something that caught you off guard. How did you find out? How long after it happened?"
- **Validation status:** [ ] Not tested  [ ] Partially validated  [ ] Validated  [ ] Invalidated

### HP-F4: No Single Source of Truth for Competitive Context
**Hypothesis:** Competitive knowledge lives in the founder's head, scattered Slack messages, and various team members' notes. When a new executive joins, there's no way to onboard them on the competitive landscape quickly.
- **Evidence from interviews:** Chanchal — "it sits somewhere in the dock." Sai Bhaskar — "spreadsheets get lost over time."
- **Testable question:** "If your VP of Sales started tomorrow, how would they learn about your competitive landscape? How long would it take them to be fully up to speed?"
- **Validation status:** [ ] Not tested  [ ] Partially validated  [ ] Validated  [ ] Invalidated

### HP-F5: Fundraising Narrative Requires Competitive Clarity
**Hypothesis:** During fundraising, founders need to clearly articulate their competitive moat and differentiation. They struggle to present a data-backed competitive analysis and often wing it.
- **Evidence from interviews:** Soubhik (DemandBase) — "Porter's Five Forces, moats" are what investors care about. Ram Rathi — "At a 300M ARR scale, even helping them win one deal makes the ROI clear."
- **Testable question:** "During your last fundraise, how did you present the competitive landscape? Did investors push back on any competitive claims? What data did you use?"
- **Validation status:** [ ] Not tested  [ ] Partially validated  [ ] Validated  [ ] Invalidated

### HP-F6: Strategic Bets Without Market Validation
**Hypothesis:** Founders make product/market expansion decisions (new verticals, new geographies, new features) without systematically analyzing what competitors are doing in those areas.
- **Evidence from interviews:** Siddharth (SaaS Labs) — "We were looking at wrong competitors... comparing ourselves with established players already far into the game."
- **Testable question:** "When you decided to enter [new market/vertical], how did you assess the competitive landscape there? Did you discover competitors you didn't expect?"
- **Validation status:** [ ] Not tested  [ ] Partially validated  [ ] Validated  [ ] Invalidated

### HP-F7: Delegation Without Verification
**Hypothesis:** Founders delegate competitive tracking to a PMM or analyst but have no way to verify completeness or quality. They suspect their team is missing things but don't have time to check.
- **Evidence from interviews:** Anjali (Anaplan) — lean CI team of 2 people. Multiple interviews showed CI is someone's part-time job, not a function.
- **Testable question:** "Who owns competitive intelligence in your org? How confident are you that they're catching everything important? What would make you more confident?"

## Buying Triggers
- Board meeting in 2-3 weeks (needs competitive slides)
- Lost a significant deal to a competitor
- Competitor raised a large round or made an acquisition
- Preparing for fundraise (need competitive positioning narrative)
- New market entry decision
- Pricing revision cycle
- Key executive hire (need onboarding material)

## How They'd Measure ROI
- Time saved on board prep (hours → minutes)
- Competitive surprises eliminated (zero blindsides per quarter)
- Win rate improvement (even 1-2% at scale = massive revenue)
- Faster strategic decision-making
- Investor confidence in competitive awareness

## Likely Objections
- "I already have a good pulse on the market from my network."
- "My PMM/analyst handles this."
- "We're not in a hyper-competitive market."
- "I can just ask ChatGPT."
- "We're too early-stage to need this."
