# Interview Extract: Garima Tiwari

**Date:** 25th February, 2026
**Role/Company:** Marketing Consultant (Freelance); currently consulting for Levo AI; previously Senior Content/SEO Lead at BrowserStack (4+ years)
**Background:** Garima is a B2B content marketing and organic growth specialist with deep experience scaling content engines at BrowserStack (from 30 pieces/quarter to 250+ pieces/month, generating 1.5M traffic). She is currently a freelance consultant helping early-stage tech companies build content-led growth strategies from scratch, with expertise in SEO, AEO/GEO, and competitive content analysis.

## Objections & Challenges Raised

### Objection: G2/Review-Sourced Content Is Too Bottom-of-Funnel
- **Quote:** "Content may when you get that bottom of the funnel kind of content that is a very niche content... we write it only when you have a substantial amount of regular viewership. But to maintain that level of traffic and viewership as well, we need to also ensure that we write top of the funnel high volume keywords."
- **Timestamp:** [07:50]
- **Severity:** Medium — doesn't reject the feature, but narrows its utility; signals that G2 review intelligence alone won't be a primary hook for content marketers
- **Category:** Value Prop
- **Summary:** Garima acknowledges that G2/Capterra reviews are useful but frames them as bottom-of-funnel content — only useful once substantial audience already exists. The primary content need is top-of-funnel, high-search-volume keyword opportunities, not niche complaint-mining. This challenges the assumption that review aggregation is a universal content win.
- **Connects to:** New — specific to content marketing persona; distinct from typical product/PMM use case

---

### Objection: Data Dashboard Feels Like an Information Dump Without Enough Intelligence
- **Quote:** "Somewhere it is in the mid Fort by building out some insights out of it. If you will build some insights from that will just flip the whole game... This is just like data visualization dashboard."
- **Timestamp:** [41:21] – [43:29]
- **Severity:** High — directly challenges the core product if it stays at data aggregation level without actionable synthesis
- **Category:** Value Prop
- **Summary:** Garima explicitly calls out the risk of the product being perceived as a dashboarding tool rather than an intelligence tool. She stresses that busy managers will take raw data and paste it into ChatGPT/Gemini themselves if the product doesn't surface clear insights and action items. The differentiation must come from the intelligence layer, not just the data collection layer.
- **Connects to:** Likely connects to other interviews where the ChatGPT/Perplexity comparison arises — "why not just use AI yourself?"

### Objection: Volume of Competitor Content Makes Tracking Infeasible — Must Be Skim-Friendly
- **Quote:** "Everybody posting one or two content every day and me keeping tab of everything becomes hectic because I have to manage my work as well... if I get the headliners or where I can skim through and understand what is important, what is not, those kind of things can be helpful for me."
- **Timestamp:** [14:39]
- **Severity:** Medium — not a rejection, but a strong signal that the product will fail adoption if it surfaces too much raw content without summarization/filtering
- **Category:** Product
- **Summary:** Garima works in a small marketing team and is already overwhelmed by manually tracking competitors. A tool that surfaces full blog posts or excessive detail will not be adopted. The format must be newsletter-style, topic-level headlines with optional drill-down. This is a UX and information architecture challenge.
- **Connects to:** Information Overload — likely pattern across small marketing team personas

## Strategic Insights

### Insight: AEO/GEO Is Actively Disrupting Content Marketing — Competitors Have No Playbook Yet
- **Quote:** "We need to move ahead from traffic and we need to move towards more AO geo. We still figuring that out... I am still finding the matrices even."
- **Timestamp:** [27:24]
- **Implication for Kompete:** There is a white space opportunity to build AEO/GEO tracking into the competitive intelligence layer — specifically tracking which competitors are ranking in AI Overviews, Perplexity citations, and featured snippets. This is an unmet need that existing tools (Semrush, Klue, Crayon) have not yet addressed, and Kompete's claim verification and content monitoring capabilities could be positioned directly against this transition.

---

### Insight: Blog Content Can Drive 40%+ of Leads in B2B Self-Serve — Making Content Intel High-Stakes
- **Quote:** "You would be surprised to know a lot of leads. 40% of the leads came from the blog itself... When you don't have that kind of money, I think organic content is the classic thing."
- **Timestamp:** [01:02:19]
- **Implication for Kompete:** For self-serve B2B SaaS companies with limited paid marketing budgets, competitive content intelligence is not a nice-to-have — it's directly tied to lead generation and revenue. Kompete should stress-test and refine its GTM narrative for this segment: content-led growth companies where winning the organic content game is existential.

---

### Insight: Competitive Intel Consumption Happens at the Individual Contributor Level — Not Just Leadership
- **Quote:** "They might also pull it from here and put it in charge GPT Gemini or somewhere else to get... these managers people are running short of time."
- **Timestamp:** [44:03]
- **Implication for Kompete:** The practical consumer of competitive intel is the busy individual contributor (content marketer, PMM, SDR) — not just the VP of Marketing or CEO. Kompete's team-specific delivery feature is strategically important, but the format must be optimized for fast consumption (skim-first, detail-on-demand). If the product doesn't serve the IC's daily workflow, they will route around it using ChatGPT.

---

### Insight: Small Marketing Teams Are the Most Underserved and Most Willing to Adopt
- **Quote:** "I found your product very good and I really want to help you and maybe you know through you other people like me who have to do the manual work just to hunt for what people are writing, how they are performing, what news coming."

- **Implication for Kompete:** Garima's enthusiasm — and her explicit identification with the "manual work" persona — confirms that small, under-resourced B2B marketing teams (1-5 person teams at $5–50M ARR companies) are a high-affinity ICP. They do not have Klue/Crayon budgets, they are already doing this manually, and they would pay for relief. Kompete's pricing target ($2–5K/yr) is well-positioned for this segment.

---

### Insight: Competitor Messaging Inconsistency Across Platforms Is an Underexplored Attack Surface
