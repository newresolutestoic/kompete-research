# Interview Extract: Rohan M V

**Date:** 4th February, 2026
**Role/Company:** Product Marketing Manager (PMM) at Inkle (US accounting & tax SaaS for startups)
**Background:** PMM at Inkle, a startup-focused US incorporation and tax compliance SaaS product. Previously worked in Sweden in international B2B sales for an industrial/manufacturing company. Works closely with a US-based sales team across time zones.

---

## Objections & Challenges Raised

### Objection: AI Signal Accuracy Is Unproven

- **Quote:** "I've tried doing this on, for example, Claude and Charge. So it is for now picking up these signals maybe sometimes not accurate. So I just want to try and understand, like, how, you know, how a team would typically use it."
- **Timestamp:** [04:12]
- **Severity:** High — directly questions core AI-powered value proposition before even seeing the product demo
- **Category:** Value Prop
- **Summary:** Rohan has already attempted to replicate competitive intelligence using general-purpose LLMs like Claude and ChatGPT and found the accuracy lacking. This signals that the primary objection isn't "why do I need this" but "why is your AI better than what I've already tried." He's a semi-technical user who has experimented, making a bland "AI-powered" pitch insufficient — the differentiation needs to be concrete and demonstrable.
- **Connects to:** ChatGPT/Perplexity comparison — recurring theme across discovery calls where prospects have DIY'd a version with general AI tools.

---

### Objection: Comprehensive Report Is Founder-Level, Not PMM-Level

- **Quote:** "I think most definitely, but I feel like it's most helpful for founders... from a marketing perspective they would want like something deeper. Let's say only in messaging or only on pricing and things like that."
- **Timestamp:** [46:27]
- **Severity:** High — directly challenges whether the product as demoed fits the PMM user persona
- **Category:** Market Fit
- **Summary:** When shown the draft strategic report (covering hiring signals, G2 sentiment, predictive intelligence, strategic timelines), Rohan's instinct was that this is a founder/executive tool, not a PMM tool. PMMs want depth on a specific domain (messaging, pricing) rather than breadth across all intelligence categories. This is a significant market fit and segmentation signal — the product may be solving for the wrong persona in its current packaging.
- **Connects to:** Buyer persona confusion — a recurring structural issue if the tool tries to serve all personas equally in a single report view.

---

### Objection: Narrow Focus Risks High Churn

- **Quote:** "I feel like the churn rate will be like a bit on the higher side if it's like super focused, especially in B2B on something... I'm paying for two, three apps that I rarely open."
- **Timestamp:** [28:02]
- **Severity:** High — touches retention and stickiness, which are existential for a SaaS product
- **Category:** Product
- **Summary:** Rohan draws on his own experience as a SaaS consumer, noting that niche tools get opened infrequently and end up being churned. He explicitly frames the competitive intelligence use case as something he'd only revisit during specific triggers (a product launch, a quarterly review), not daily. This raises the question of whether the tool has enough hooks to drive habitual engagement, or whether it needs broader utility to justify subscription cost.
- **Connects to:** Tool Fragmentation / Value Prop — similar to objections about ROI justification for tools used only occasionally.

---

### Objection: Bootstrapped / Early-Stage Companies Won't Pay

- **Quote:** "When you're bootstrap, right, you already have like shortage of money... you don't want to spend... apart from ChatGPT Gemini already you are paying, right? So after that paying another like say we have to price it for at least 100 or 200 for sure."
- **Timestamp:** [19:05]
- **Severity:** Medium — raised by the interviewer (Aditya) not Rohan, but Rohan validated the concern without pushing back
- **Category:** Pricing
- **Summary:** The founder himself acknowledged that pricing competitive intelligence at even $100–200/month may be a barrier for bootstrapped or early-stage companies when stacked against existing AI tool subscriptions. Rohan neither agreed nor disagreed strongly, suggesting he sees the pricing friction as real but doesn't view it as a dealbreaker for companies of Inkle's stage. Still, this narrows the addressable market and reinforces targeting $5M+ ARR companies with budget.
- **Connects to:** Pricing — aligned with Kompete's own target of $2–5K/yr at a fraction of Klue/Crayon costs.

---

### Objection: Engagement / Return Visits Need Active Design

- **Quote:** "What can you do to bring them to that particular page more often? I think that is where I think you could look at."
- **Timestamp:** [48:17]
- **Severity:** Medium — framed as a product design concern rather than a dealbreaker
- **Category:** Product
- **Summary:** Rohan observed that competitive intelligence tools are typically only consulted reactively (before a launch, during a quarterly review), not proactively. He challenged the team to think about what mechanisms would make users return to the product habitually. This is a product engagement/retention design challenge — the tool needs active triggers like alerts, ranked leaderboards, or digests to pull users back in rather than waiting for them to remember to visit.
- **Connects to:** New — specific to engagement loop design.

## Strategic Insights

### Insight: The CEO Is the De Facto Competitive Intelligence Function

- **Quote:** "Anand, our CEO is like extremely active on what our competitors are doing. So he's the first one to sniff anything and then he'll keep sending us. But yeah, again, it's very manual."
- **Implication for Kompete:** In growth-stage startups, the buyer and the primary "user" of competitive intel is often the CEO — not the PMM. Sales motion should target founders/CEOs first, who then champion the tool to their teams. The PMM may be a champion but rarely the budget owner.

---

### Insight: B2B Purchase Decisions Are Made Before the Prospect Enters the Market

- **Quote:** "Before a decision on a purchase of a particular service or a product is made, like, even before they start, you know, thinking of that, they need the product. Like they already have like a couple of companies in mind."
- **Implication for Kompete:** Competitive intelligence isn't just about knowing what competitors offer — it's about tracking how competitors are building brand awareness in pre-intent audiences. This reframes Kompete's value from "feature tracking tool" to "brand positioning intelligence platform," which is a stronger and more strategic pitch to PMMs and CMOs.

---

### Insight: Partnerships Are a Primary Growth Driver and an Intelligence Blind Spot

- **Quote:** "Most of our new customers come from partnerships... So through them we get a lot of incorporation customers. Like that is what drives."
- **Implication for Kompete:** Competitor partnership tracking (who they partner with, what discounts they offer, which ecosystems they plug into) is a high-value and largely unserved intelligence category. Adding a "Partner Ecosystem" module could be a meaningful differentiator, particularly for GTM-focused buyers.

---

### Insight: GEO Is Already a Budget Line Item — Integration Is a Consolidation Opportunity

- **Quote:** "We have been using a different tool for that [GEO tracking]... I mean if you, I mean I, I don't know if it's very complicated to actually have in this."
- **Implication for Kompete:** Rohan is already paying for Swiftly for GEO tracking. Bundling GEO ranking, Reddit monitoring, and competitive messaging intelligence into one platform is a consolidation play — not a new category sell. This lowers adoption friction significantly.

---

### Insight: Startups Treat Larger Industry Leaders as Messaging Benchmarks

- **Quote:** "Most startups in the stages that we are usually they look up to, you know, other bigger companies that are solving this. Right. At a bigger scale. And then for them that is always like one of the holy grail."
- **Implication for Kompete:** The competitive set to monitor isn't just direct competitors — it includes category-defining leaders that serve as messaging aspiration targets. Kompete should allow users to track "aspirational competitors" (e.g., larger players in the same category) in addition to direct rivals, which expands the utility of the platform.

---

### Insight: Predictive Intelligence Is Most Valuable to Founders, Not Individual Contributor Teams

- **Quote:** "I think most definitely, but I feel like it's most helpful for founders. Like it depends on the stage of the company... in growth companies like in our size we are speaking every day. So if they report even once a week, like that should drive the conversation."
- **Implication for Kompete:** The rich strategic report with predictive intelligence and confidence scores is a CEO/founder product, ideally delivered as a weekly digest that prompts cross-functional conversations. For ICs (PMMs, PMs), a narrower, domain-specific view is more immediately actionable. Segmenting these experiences may be key to adoption across the org.

## Persona Signals

- **Buyer or User?** User — Rohan would be a day-to-day user, but budget decisions would go through Anand (CEO). Rohan could be a strong internal champion but is not the economic buyer.
- **Willingness to pay:** Moderate — values the tool conceptually and is already paying for Swiftly (~similar category). Would need a compelling ROI story and likely a free trial or freemium hook to get personally invested before recommending upward. Expressed concern about tool sprawl at the $100–200/month range.
- **Decision process:** Bottom-up champion model — Rohan would test, validate, then recommend to CEO (Anand). Mentioned explicitly he'd be happy to connect the interviewer with Anand. Free trial or freemium tier is the critical entry point for this archetype.
- **Current tools mentioned:** Notion (competitor tracking), LinkedIn (social monitoring), Atio (CRM), Apollo (sales prospecting), Swiftly (GEO/AI citation tracking), Midbound (website visitor identification & enrichment), WhatsApp/Slack (internal comms), ChatGPT/Claude (ad-hoc competitive research).
