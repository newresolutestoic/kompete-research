**Date:** 12th February, 2026
**Role/Company:** Associate Strategy & Competitive Intelligence Analyst, Anaplan (San Francisco-based, remote from Delhi)
**Background:** Anjali moved from High Radius ~1.5 years ago into a hybrid GTM Strategy/CI role at Anaplan, working with a lean 2-person India-based CI team. ## Objections & Challenges Raised

### Objection: Pricing data is fundamentally untrustworthy at the enterprise level
- **Quote:** "Pricing is something you cannot find or you cannot trust with at any point."
- **Timestamp:** [07:36]
- **Severity:** Critical — directly attacks a founding pillar of the original product thesis (pricing intelligence)

She implies that even win/loss-derived pricing averages are inaccurate. This undermines the core promise of pricing intelligence as a feature.
- **Connects to:** New — but consistent with the pivot Prateek himself describes (away from pricing-only toward full-spectrum CI)

## Strategic Insights

### Insight: Challenger brands use SEO comparison pages to manufacture competitive presence — incumbents ignore this at their peril
- **Quote:** "High Radius with Anaplan, High Radius with BlackLine. And they're building all of these pages and putting it on their website. Even if they are doing it, High Radius will come up as a competitor for Anaplan. Like if somebody is researching, right — they are making sure that this appears."
- **Implication for Kompete:** AEO/SEO monitoring of who is creating "vs. [your brand]" pages is a critical and underappreciated use case. Kompete's AEO tracking feature is directly relevant here, and should be pitched as a threat early-warning system — incumbents routinely ignore challenger SEO attacks until it's too late.

---

### Insight: "Hiring ex-employees from competitors" is the industry's primary CI method — it's the incumbent that Kompete is replacing
- **Quote:** "That is the exact strategy that everyone is following. Okay. So, you know, they'll be providing us the insider information and that's how we get to know what, at what place they are."
- **Implication for Kompete:** The real competitive alternative isn't Klue or Crayon — it's the informal human network. Kompete's positioning should acknowledge this directly and show how it systematizes and scales what a well-connected insider employee provides, without the hiring cost and attrition risk.

---

### Insight: CI practitioners frame tool value in headcount terms, not SaaS pricing benchmarks
- **Quote:** "This can reduce me my 25% of headcount in my team is what I feel. 25 to 30%."
- **Implication for Kompete:** ROI messaging should lead with headcount efficiency (e.g., "do the work of 2 analysts with 1") rather than comparison to Klue/Crayon pricing. For lean teams like Anjali's (2 people), the pitch is essentially: this tool lets you not hire your next analyst.

---

### Insight: Enterprise CI teams are dangerously small and about to grow — creating a buying window
- **Quote:** "She is planning to expand the team. Take care of a bit of marketing also under her."
- **Implication for Kompete:** A new director at a target account expanding a lean CI team is a high-signal buying trigger. Kompete's GTM should monitor leadership hires in CI/strategy/marketing roles at ICP accounts as a prospecting signal — the moment a new head of CI is hired, that's the window to pitch before headcount is locked in.

---

### Insight: Incumbents deliberately ignore smaller competitors — creating a discovery blind spot Kompete can exploit as a demo hook
- **Quote:** "Marketing team is aware of High Radius, but they do not give any importance. Like people do not ask the competitive intelligence team about High Radius. So..." / "Anaplan cannot say that outside obviously... Anaplan doesn't feel like High Radius is even important to them."
- **Implication for Kompete:** The demo should open with "here's who's attacking you that you don't know about" — surfacing a real, live competitor that the CI team has dismissed. This creates an immediate aha moment and bypasses the "we already track competitors manually" objection.

---

### Insight: Pricing opacity is a deliberate enterprise sales tactic, not a data gap — Kompete should reframe around it
- **Quote:** "That's just their tactic to, you know, attract the lead and then they'll go ahead and add their add-ons or they'll start, maybe they'll cut down price."
- **Implication for Kompete:** For enterprise-focused CI teams, Kompete should stop leading with pricing intelligence and instead lead with feature velocity, review sentiment, hiring signals, and messaging drift. Pricing intelligence is a valid feature for PLG/mid-market ICP but will be dismissed as noise by enterprise CI practitioners like Anjali.
