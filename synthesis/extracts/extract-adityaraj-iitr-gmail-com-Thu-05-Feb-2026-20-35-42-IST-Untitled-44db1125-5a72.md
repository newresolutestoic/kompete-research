# Interview Extract: Sankalp Srivastava

**Date:** 5th February, 2026
**Role/Company:** Product Marketing Manager, HighRadius (R2R / Record-to-Report product line)
**Background:** Sankalp handled competitive intelligence solo for HighRadius's R2R product, responsible for battle cards, competitor tables, landing pages, and MOFU/BOFU content strategy. He previously worked under a lead who departed, leaving him as the sole owner of competitive intelligence for his product line.

---

## Objections & Challenges Raised

### Objection: One Person + AI Is Already Enough

- **Quote:** "With AI and all, one person is enough to handle for all the [competitors]. If you ask me how much time it would take, for starters it would take a little bit of time… but once he does it for one competitor, it should be like a two-day task."
- **Timestamp:** [09:09]
- **Severity:** High
- **Category:** Value Prop
- **Summary:** Sankalp's direct experience suggests that a single marketer armed with AI can produce competitive deliverables across multiple competitors efficiently. This implicitly challenges Kompete's value proposition — if AI-augmented humans can do this at low marginal cost, why pay for a platform? The "two days for all competitors" framing undercuts urgency.
- **Connects to:** New — but relates to the ChatGPT/free-tool objection (see below)

---

### Objection: ChatGPT Is a Strong Free Alternative

- **Quote:** "Those who use ChatGPT extensively will think… ours has to be superlative every time otherwise they will just come back and say [why pay]."
- **Timestamp:** [01:09:17] (Prateek's post-interview debrief)
- **Severity:** Critical
- **Category:** Differentiation
- **Summary:** Prateek himself surfaces this as a core risk during the debrief: users who are already proficient with ChatGPT will default to it unless Kompete's output is demonstrably, consistently superior. This is a table-stakes differentiation challenge that the team explicitly flagged as unresolved. The bar isn't "better sometimes" — it's "superlative every time."
- **Connects to:** Common objection pattern across AI-native tools; likely to surface in other interviews

---

### Objection: More Research Is an Overkill — Scope Must Be Narrow

- **Quote:** "More than this would be an overkill because you can't even, like, over-detail your things, or else nobody's gonna read it."
- **Timestamp:** [23:21]
- **Severity:** Medium
- **Category:** Product
- **Summary:** Sankalp believes competitive intelligence for product launches should be scoped narrowly: features, ad copies, messaging — and nothing more. He explicitly resists comprehensive monitoring as excessive. This challenges Kompete's 24/7 all-signal monitoring pitch; the buyer may perceive breadth as noise, not value.
- **Connects to:** Information Overload pain point; pricing/value justification

---

### Objection: New Competitors Don't Need Tracking

- **Quote:** "If it's a new competitor, our prospects also won't know it. Competitor tables — we can be differentiable against our prospect… but with a new person coming in, I don't think so they are like on the map."
- **Timestamp:** [12:05]
- **Severity:** Medium
- **Category:** Market Fit
- **Summary:** Sankalp's mental model is that competitive intelligence is only valuable when the competitor is already known to prospects. This limits the perceived value of proactive early-warning signals — a core Kompete differentiator — unless framed as product strategy input (which Prateek pushes back on effectively).
- **Connects to:** New — ties to the "paranoid survivor" framing Aditya uses at [01:13:52]

---

### Objection: Competitive Research Findings Often Match Public Marketing Collateral Anyway

- **Quote:** "No, this was pretty much the same thing." (When asked if call listening revealed insights materially different from competitors' own marketing collateral)
- **Timestamp:** [18:17]
- **Severity:** Medium
- **Category:** Value Prop
- **Summary:** When asked whether deep qualitative research (50–150 call reviews) surfaced anything meaningfully different from what competitors publish publicly, Sankalp said the findings largely matched. This is a soft objection to deep intelligence: if public data is sufficient, automated monitoring of public signals may be all that's needed — but it also inadvertently validates Kompete's scraping-of-public-signals approach.
- **Connects to:** New

---

### Objection: Legal Risk of Direct Competitor Name Usage Limits Utility

- **Quote:** "You need to make sure you are not directly taking their name. At least BlackLine… BlackLine and Flokas, they send a lot of lawsuits."
- **Timestamp:** [02:26]
- **Severity:** Low
- **Category:** GTM
- **Summary:** There is a legal constraint on how competitive intelligence outputs can actually be used in external marketing. Battle cards created from Kompete data would be for internal use only — not publishable as-is. This doesn't challenge Kompete's core value but is a real-world friction point for downstream activation of intel.
- **Connects to:** New

## Strategic Insights

### Insight: Competitor Tables Are MOFU Assets — Not Just Sales Collateral

- **Quote:** "Competitor tables are MOFU. So on the landing pages you can write a copy… find out about it through a demo. But the competitor table which is being downloaded — that's the middle of the funnel asset."
- **Implication for Kompete:** Kompete's output (battle cards, competitor comparisons) should be positioned not just as sales enablement but as demand generation and pipeline acceleration tools. This reframes the ROI story: Kompete drives MQLs, not just win rates.

---

### Insight: Product Marketing Owns MOFU/BOFU — Not Sales — Making Them the Primary Buyer

- **Quote:** "MOFU content and BOFU content is written and strategized by us — Product Marketing. Tofu majorly is being handled by content marketing."
- **Implication for Kompete:** The ICP for Kompete at companies like HighRadius is Product Marketing, not Sales Enablement or Sales Ops. GTM outreach should be targeted at Product Marketing Managers and Directors, who own battle cards and competitor tables as direct job deliverables.

---

### Insight: Gartner Magic Quadrant Placement Is a Tier-1 Competitive Event

- **Quote:** "Once we got like on the Gartner Magic Quadrant leader thing — once we like they started noticing us — then we got like, okay, these guys are coming up, we need to make a competitor table."
- **Implication for Kompete:** Analyst ranking changes (Gartner, G2, Forrester) are treated as existential competitive signals. Kompete should build automated alerts for analyst report changes and quadrant movements — this is a high-value, underserved signal category.

---

### Insight: The "Paranoid Survivor" Framing Resonates as Positioning

- **Quote:** "Only the paranoid survives. For paranoid teams who want to survive — yeah, marketing."
- **Implication for Kompete:** Aditya's invocation of Andy Grove's "only the paranoid survive" framing landed well in this context. Kompete could use "built for paranoid teams" as a resonant positioning angle for aggressive, competitive-market B2B SaaS companies.

---

### Insight: Sales Lost Deals Without Knowing the Competitive Scenario — and Nobody Flagged It

- **Quote:** "Basically we never knew [the] compete scenario. So that was a big flag."
- **Implication for Kompete:** Even at a $1B+ revenue company like HighRadius, deal loss analysis is not systematically tied to competitive intelligence. This is a systemic gap — and Kompete's sub-3-minute alerts and auto-updating battle cards directly address the root cause (stale, inaccessible intel at the moment of truth).

- **Buyer or User?** Primarily a **User** — Sankalp is an individual Product Marketing contributor who would use the tool daily. He is not a budget owner. The likely buyer at his level of org would be VP of Product Marketing or CMO.
- **Willingness to pay:** Not directly addressed. Sankalp currently operates as a one-person team using free/included tools (ChatGPT, Gemini, Clarity, G2). No signals of existing paid competitive intelligence budget. The team likely has no Klue/Crayon subscription.
- **Decision process:** Not discussed directly. Given his IC role, he would be an influencer/champion, not a decision-maker. The tool would need to demonstrably reduce his workload and improve MQL output to generate internal advocacy.
