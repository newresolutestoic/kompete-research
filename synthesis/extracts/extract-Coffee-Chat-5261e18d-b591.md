# Interview Extract: Vidushi Duhan

**Date:** February 11, 2026
**Role/Company:** Product Manager, Razor Labs (B2B SaaS, Martech/hyperlocal domain; ~120 employees across three sister companies)
**Background:** Nearly 3 years at Razor Labs building and managing a hyperlocal Martech platform for enterprise retail brands. Previously co-founded a consumer hyperlocal application. Has end-to-end exposure to competitive analysis from ideation through ongoing product development.

---

## Objections & Challenges Raised

### Objection: Positioning Drift Won't Drive Product Decisions
- **Quote:** "If you tell me that single interface is changing its positioning. Of course I would not take any action on my product because of that. My positioning is something not dependent on my competitors positioning for sure."
- **Timestamp:** [37:06]
- **Severity:** High — directly challenges one of Kompete's headline features (Strategy Timeline / positioning drift tracking)
- **Category:** Value Prop
- **Summary:** Vidushi believes competitor positioning changes are interesting but not actionable from a product strategy standpoint. She treats her own positioning as an independent decision, not a reactive one. This suggests the value of positioning drift monitoring may land more clearly with marketing teams than with product managers.
- **Connects to:** New — but echoes a broader pattern of buyers questioning whether intelligence translates to action

---

### Objection: Tool Is Most Useful for One-Time Analysis, Not Ongoing Monitoring
- **Quote:** "Very like initially when you do one time analysis at that time this can be very helpful. Yeah. When I am trying to make my positioning."
- **Timestamp:** [37:50]
- **Severity:** High — implies the recurring subscription model may be hard to justify for her use case; she frames CI as episodic, not continuous
- **Category:** Value Prop / GTM
- **Summary:** Vidushi frames competitive analysis as primarily a pre-launch or ideation-phase activity. She describes rich, structured analysis done at key decision points (build/no-build, B2B vs. B2C, product-led vs. sales-led), but her ongoing monitoring is light and reactive. She doesn't articulate a felt need for 24/7 monitoring.
- **Connects to:** Potential pattern with other interviewees who distinguish between "research mode" and "tracking mode" CI needs

---

### Objection: Sales-Led Companies Can't Access Competitor Products — and Kompete Can't Solve This
- **Quote:** "I would still struggle with you know something if I'm sales driven because we are sales led... we don't get to know how our competitors product look."
- **Timestamp:** [38:12]
- **Severity:** Critical — she identifies a fundamental pain point that Kompete explicitly cannot address, and Aditya acknowledged it: "I'm not sure if we'll be able to solve it, by the way that we are kind of envisioning."
- **Category:** Product / Market Fit
- **Summary:** For sales-led companies, the most valuable competitive insight — what the competitor's actual product does inside — is inaccessible through public signals. Kompete's approach (public data aggregation) hits a ceiling here. Vidushi validated this as an active, ongoing challenge that no tool currently solves.
- **Connects to:** Validated by a HackerRank PM in a separate conversation mentioned by Aditya — this is a recurring cross-interview signal

---

### Objection: Existing Comparison Platforms Are Untrustworthy (Paid/Biased)
- **Quote:** "Those are all paid. So when they did the analysis, we rejected all of that analysis because we knew that part is paid. So that tool, we don't trust it."
- **Timestamp:** [29:20]
- **Severity:** Medium — not a direct objection to Kompete, but signals that data provenance and trust will be table-stakes purchase criteria
- **Category:** Differentiation
- **Summary:** Vidushi's team explicitly discarded analysis from G2/Capterra-style comparison pages because they assumed the rankings and write-ups were pay-to-play. This creates an opportunity for Kompete to differentiate on data integrity, but also sets a high trust bar: buyers will interrogate where data comes from.
- **Connects to:** New — potential pattern with other buyers who distrust aggregated review platforms

## Strategic Insights

### Insight: CI Needs Are Stage-Dependent, Not Role-Dependent
- **Quote:** "For different product stages we had different type of, you know, framework to do competitor analysis."
- **Implication for Kompete:** Kompete's platform may need to serve meaningfully different workflows depending on whether a customer is pre-launch (market sizing, build/no-build), post-MVP (ongoing feature tracking), or scaling (sales battlecards, hiring signals). A one-size-fits-all dashboard risks feeling irrelevant to where each customer actually is.

---

### Insight: Social Listening Is the Current Alert System — But It's Passive and Laggy
- **Quote:** "We get to know that some activity has happened and then when that part happens, then we check their website."
- **Implication for Kompete:** The current workaround is essentially "wait for a signal, then investigate manually." Kompete's sub-3-minute alert system directly compresses this lag — but the pitch should make explicit that Kompete *replaces* the follow-on manual investigation, not just the initial notification.

---

### Insight: Sales-Led vs. Product-Led Is a Fundamental Segmentation Variable for CI Value
- **Quote:** "In sales we still have that challenge. We don't get to know how our competitors product look. Yeah."
- **Implication for Kompete:** Sales-led companies have the highest pain around competitor product access — but that's also the hardest problem to solve with public data alone. Product-led companies can buy competitor subscriptions; sales-led cannot. Kompete should consider whether its ICP should skew toward product-led SaaS, or invest in solving the sales-led problem uniquely (e.g., via community-sourced product screenshots, Gong/call intelligence integrations).

---

### Insight: Trusted, Primary-Source Data Is a Differentiator That Must Be Explicitly Messaged
- **Quote:** "Those are all paid. So when they did the analysis, we rejected all of that analysis because we knew that part is paid. So that tool, we don't trust it."
- **Implication for Kompete:** Buyers are actively skeptical of aggregated, potentially sponsored competitive data. Kompete should proactively communicate its data sourcing methodology — emphasizing that insights are derived from raw public signals (job postings, patents, Reddit, web crawls), not curated/paid listings. This is a trust wedge against G2, Gartner Peer Insights, etc.

---

### Insight: Competitive Analysis Is Used to Make Major Strategic Bets, Not Just Track Competitors
- **Quote:** "We wanted to know what happened because they anyway had such a brand leverage... So why did they move from B2C to B2B? So all of those analysis we had to do."
- **Implication for Kompete:** CI isn't just about day-to-day competitor tracking — it informs major directional bets (market entry, go-to-market model, product scope). Kompete could position not just as a monitoring tool but as a strategic decision support system, especially for founders and PMs evaluating pivots.

- **Buyer or User?** Primarily a **user** — she makes product decisions but likely does not control the software budget unilaterally. Could be an internal champion.
- **Willingness to pay:** No explicit pricing signals. Her company is a hybrid service/product shop (~120 employees), which may mean tighter discretionary software budgets. She did not push back on pricing — but she also didn't ask about it, suggesting she's in "evaluation" mode, not "buying" mode.
- **Decision process:** Not discussed directly. Given the company structure (three sister companies, service + product), a purchase would likely involve her manager or a shared budget owner. She mentioned having a hard stop for another call — time-constrained, efficient communicator.
- **Current tools mentioned:**
  - Social media follows / newsletter subscriptions (primary alert mechanism)
  - Direct competitor website monitoring
  - YouTube channel analysis (competitor demo videos)
  - Google search (competitor comparisons)
  - G2/Capterra-style comparison pages (explicitly distrusted and rejected)
  - No dedicated CI tool in active use
