# Interview Extract: Harsh Kumar Singh

**Date:** 10th February, 2026
**Role/Company:** Product Manager at Turbo (Broker-centric Transportation Management System, US-focused)
**Background:** IIT Delhi graduate (2014), MBA via CAT (2017), 6–7 years in product management with a background in supply chain consulting. Currently leads product at Turbo, a North America–focused TMS platform serving freight brokers, with ~8–10 competitors including Oracle E2 Open and Mercury Gate.

---

## Objections & Challenges Raised

### Objection: B2B Pricing Lives Behind Paywalls

- **Quote:** "B2B platforms if you must have seen are mostly behind the paywall. All right. And if you want to access anything you have to go for the either their trial product... only in the discovery calls with certain customer you get to feel that this is what they're going for."
- **Timestamp:** [07:25]
- **Severity:** High — directly limits the pricing intelligence pillar of Kompete's core value prop
- **Category:** Value Prop
- **Summary:** Harsh argues that in B2B SaaS, pricing and deep feature details are rarely publicly available. Most intelligence comes through active sales interactions rather than passive web monitoring. This constrains how much of Kompete's value can actually be delivered at scale, particularly for enterprise-motion companies that never publish pricing.
- **Connects to:** Pricing Opacity — likely raised by other interviewees in enterprise B2B verticals

---

### Objection: Deep Feature-Level Intelligence Requires Human Access, Not Just Web Scraping

- **Quote:** "But at the execution level, what they are actually building, how is their feature performing? Or let's say with this particular module, let's say one of the company hired 300 odd data annotators. That means they are working on the training module, right? But which specific feature they are working that becomes a key thing somebody has to look out for."
- **Timestamp:** [08:23]
- **Severity:** High — challenges whether AI-driven web intelligence can go beyond surface-level signals
- **Category:** Value Prop
- **Summary:** Harsh draws a clear distinction between high-level directional signals (e.g., hiring AI engineers → building AI product) and specific feature-level intelligence, which only comes from direct human interaction with competitor sales teams or customers. He is skeptical that publicly available data alone can close that gap, and Aditya concedes this limitation during the exchange.
- **Connects to:** Raised implicitly by the "claim verification" differentiator in Kompete's positioning — this objection sharpens why that matters

---

### Objection: Unclear GTM Positioning — Sector vs. Workflow vs. Company

- **Quote:** "So if you're offering a competitive intelligence platform, how are you going to... will it be sector basis, workflow basis. Have you identified certain workflows where we can say we are offering competitive intelligence on let's say E Signature or let's say reverse logistics or let's say first mile, maybe round optimization?"
- **Timestamp:** [17:10]
- **Severity:** High — repeated 3+ times; suggests the product narrative is not yet crisp enough for a warm prospect to immediately understand their entry point
- **Category:** GTM
- **Summary:** Harsh, speaking "from an outsider view," repeatedly probes how Kompete would be packaged and pitched — by industry vertical, by workflow, or by company. He doesn't receive a fully satisfying answer. Aditya acknowledges customization will be needed early on. This reveals that without a clear ICP wedge story, prospects will struggle to see themselves as the target customer.
- **Connects to:** New — unique framing from a supply chain / workflow-centric perspective; not a standard SaaS objection

---

### Objection: Data Conflict Risk When Competitors Are Both Customers

- **Quote:** "Let's say if I want to do research for Black Line and I also want to do research for High Radius. But if I'm getting both the information in one view, why would a paper too. So hiding information. Right. Becomes very important in this case."
- **Timestamp:** [47:31]
- **Severity:** Medium — a product/trust architecture concern, not a deal-breaker yet, but Aditya doesn't have a satisfying answer
- **Category:** Product
- **Summary:** Harsh identifies a nuanced but important concern: if two competing companies both use Kompete, they're drawing from the same underlying data pool. Even if POV-framing changes, the raw data symmetry is a potential trust and differentiation issue. He frames it as an information-hiding/isolation problem that will need to be solved before enterprise adoption.
- **Connects to:** New — likely becomes more pressing as Kompete acquires customers in the same vertical

---

### Objection: Trade Shows and Physical Events Can't Be Fully Automated

- **Quote:** "Trade shows are also very important. Unless somebody puts a video of trade show on the YouTube. Right. Which they do, but it's not detailed. Let's say if it's a 2D event you will probably get a 12 to 15 minute video on YouTube."
- **Timestamp:** [36:31]
- **Severity:** Medium — not a core challenge to Kompete's value, but flags a gap in coverage for physical-economy verticals
- **Category:** Product
- **Summary:** In the TMS / logistics space, trade shows (e.g., Manifest in Las Vegas) are one of the PRIMARY competitive intelligence channels. These events yield deep demo and feature-level intelligence that isn't captured digitally. Kompete's current automated monitoring scope doesn't address this, which means it would cover only a subset of this persona's CI needs.
- **Connects to:** New — vertical-specific; likely not an issue for pure digital SaaS buyers

---

### Objection: Predictive Intelligence Data Accuracy Must Be "Spot On"

- **Quote:** "Yeah, the numbers have to be really spot on. So probably we can see if we can get data around that from say a Gartner or research companies."
- **Timestamp:** [49:58]
- **Severity:** Medium — echoes a broader concern about LLM hallucination and data reliability in a business-critical context
- **Category:** Value Prop
- **Summary:** When Harsh suggests including TAM and macroeconomic data layers, Aditya's response reveals a data accuracy constraint. Harsh's qualifier that numbers need to be "spot on" signals that if the platform presents strategic intelligence with errors, it could undermine trust in the entire system. This connects to Kompete's stated "claim verification" differentiator — users will expect high accuracy on actionable insights.
- **Connects to:** Claim verification differentiator; likely raised in other interviews focused on LLM reliability

## Pain Points Validated

## Strategic Insights

### Insight: Physical Economy B2B Verticals Have a Different CI Playbook

- **Quote:** "TMS is a more of a laggard industry when it comes to technology adoption as compared to let's say any other industry... Transportation will be always a laggard because it's a physical economy. Right. Physical economy always lags behind in adopting the technology."
- **Timestamp:** [32:40]
- **Implication for Kompete:** Physical-economy B2B SaaS buyers (logistics, supply chain, manufacturing) rely heavily on offline channels (trade shows, roadshows) for competitive intelligence — channels that Kompete currently can't monitor. Positioning to these verticals may require a hybrid approach or honest scoping of what's covered.

---

### Insight: Trade Shows Are a Primary — Not Supplementary — CI Channel in Logistics

- **Quote:** "So we have recently an event coming up in US called Manifest. It's in Las Vegas where you will be having your exhibition along with all the other TMS. So that becomes one key source of insight. So they demo the product."
- **Timestamp:** [33:47]
- **Implication for Kompete:** For sub-verticals like TMS, logistics, and supply chain, a CI platform that only monitors digital sources covers perhaps 50–60% of where intelligence actually flows. This is a meaningful coverage gap for this persona and should either be scoped out explicitly or addressed via community/event data partnerships.

---

### Insight: Prospect Sales Cycles Are Themselves a Competitive Intelligence Source

- **Quote:** "When we reach out to that particular lead they will say hey, we are also evaluating this particular platform. And then the sales team will say hey, why don't just do an analysis... When you do that analysis we get more insight on what they offer, what are the gaps."
- **Timestamp:** [34:35]
- **Implication for Kompete:** B2B companies rely on live deals to surface competitor intelligence in real time. This "deal-driven CI" is reactive and unstructured — a strong Kompete use case is to proactively surface the same intel before sales has to discover it through a lost deal or competitive RFP. This framing could sharpen Kompete's pitch to sales leaders.

---

### Insight: Annual Product Strategy Reviews Are the Current "System" — Creating a Dangerous Lag

- **Quote:** "Every year we start our product strategy from that actually... annually we do have a look obviously by the books if we say annually. But let's say something urgent comes up... A big customer offering us 100k or 200k and they are saying hey, your competitor has this."
- **Timestamp:** [39:28]
- **Implication for Kompete:** The dominant competitive intelligence cadence in mid-market B2B SaaS is annual, triggered ad hoc only by large-deal pressure. Kompete's sub-3-minute alerts and continuous monitoring represent a fundamental upgrade to this rhythm — this is a strong contrast to use in sales narratives and onboarding.

---

### Insight: The "Google vs. ChatGPT Blindsiding" Framing Resonates as an Urgency Hook

- **Quote:** "Otherwise another ChatGPT moment will happen to what happened with Google. Right. They were blindsided by... they didn't knew that ChatGPT might launch something that will take our Google Ads which they were totally not concerned about at all." / Harsh: "I really like you mentioned AI members because no one knows what's happens."
- **Timestamp:** [23:18–23:45]
- **Implication for Kompete:** This narrative framing creates genuine emotional resonance with product and strategy leaders. It should be a standard element of Kompete's pitch deck and outbound messaging — it connects the abstract value of CI monitoring to a widely-felt, high-stakes industry moment.

## Persona Signals

- **Buyer or User?** User and champion — Harsh is a Product Manager who would actively use the platform and advocate internally, but budget decisions would sit with a VP of Product, Head of Strategy, or C-suite. He explicitly says "discuss internally" before committing to a POC.
- **Willingness to pay:** No explicit price discussion. Enthusiasm is genuine ("I really like the product") but cautious — he needs to build an internal case. His validation of the E-Signature research pain (6–7 vendors contacted manually) is a strong WTP signal if the ROI can be framed in time saved.

- **Current tools mentioned:** Gainsight, LinkedIn, YouTube (competitor demo videos), competitor help desks/release notes, trade shows (Manifest, Las Vegas), customer roadshows, internal seminars, India Brand Equity Foundation (IBEF) for macro data, QuickBooks (prior use)
