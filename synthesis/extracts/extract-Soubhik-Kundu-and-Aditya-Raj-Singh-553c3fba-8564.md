# Interview Extract: Soubhik Kundu

**Date:** 7th February, 2026
**Role/Company:** Senior Product Manager (formerly at Demand Base); also has startup experience; based in Bangalore, India
**Background:** Experienced B2B PM with hands-on competitive analysis experience at Demand Base, where he competed against ZoomInfo and 6sense. Has run a startup and conducted research with ~100 US startup sales leaders on review practices.

---

## Objections & Challenges Raised

### Objection: Product Teams Barely Use Competitive Intel
- **Quote:** "If your primary Persona is a product manager, then I think it matters. But it's 5% of the equation because whenever I build a product or a feature or something, I try to see like what others have in that field. But mostly like 90% of the time, maybe 95% of the time my decisions will not matter — like my decisions will not be based on what other people are doing."
- **Timestamp:** [00:00] – [01:10]
- **Severity:** Critical — directly challenges the product team use case that Kompete.ai has been pitching
- **Category:** Market Fit
- **Summary:** A senior PM explicitly states that competitive intelligence drives at most 5-10% of product decisions. Most product work is inward-looking — talking to customers, building moats — not watching competitors. Targeting PMs as a primary persona would therefore produce low engagement and low perceived value.
- **Connects to:** Likely consistent across PM personas in other interviews; classic "I don't use this" objection pattern

---

### Objection: Cannot Personally See the Product Use Case
- **Quote:** "For me to be very honest, like being a product person, it's not — I cannot see the use case which you are trying to show me. That is where I'm coming from."
- **Timestamp:** [33:55]
- **Severity:** Critical — this is a trained PM with startup experience failing to self-identify as a user
- **Category:** Value Prop
- **Summary:** Despite being familiar with competitive intelligence workflows and having performed manual competitor tracking himself, Soubhik cannot connect Kompete.ai's vision to a recurring job-to-be-done he personally has. This suggests the all-company positioning may dilute the narrative and obscure the strongest use case.
- **Connects to:** Persona confusion — same as the "who exactly is this for?" objection pattern

---

### Objection: B2B Feature Launch Tracking Not a Real Pain
- **Quote:** "I don't think launch will be a big deal... I don't think for B2B it's a big pain."
- **Timestamp:** [30:11] – [30:24]
- **Severity:** High — challenges a core monitoring feature
- **Category:** Product
- **Summary:** Soubhik argues that in B2B, customers are close to their vendors, product roadmaps are shared in advance with key accounts, and CSMs telegraph feature launches. This makes real-time feature launch alerts far less urgent than in B2C contexts.
- **Connects to:** New — specific to B2B product launch monitoring

---

### Objection: ROI Cannot Be Easily Justified
- **Quote:** "How much value you are creating for your customers — don't go by this 'okay these all intelligence, we are giving intelligence' — how that is converting into dollar values. Think about that and then build those things."
- **Timestamp:** [21:54] – [22:52]
- **Severity:** High — buyers will demand ROI proof before renewing or expanding
- **Category:** Value Prop
- **Summary:** Without a clear feedback loop showing that competitive intel led to a won deal, a saved customer, or a product decision, Soubhik predicts that willingness to pay will erode quickly. He specifically recommends building an analytics layer that attributes revenue outcomes to Kompete.ai's signals.
- **Connects to:** ROI justification — common objection pattern in competitive intelligence tools

---

### Objection: B2B Reviews Are Unreliable Data
- **Quote:** "Reviews are mostly flawed to be very honest... we talked to almost 100 startup sales leaders and these startups were in the US — almost 70, 80% people said like we pay our customers, once they convert, we pay them to write a review for us."
- **Timestamp:** [08:59] – [10:34]
- **Severity:** Medium — challenges the reliability of a data source Kompete.ai likely uses
- **Category:** Product
- **Summary:** If Kompete.ai ingests G2/Capterra reviews as a signal, a trained PM who has researched this directly will question the data quality. He noted only negative reviews are trustworthy because positive ones are frequently incentivized.
- **Connects to:** Data quality / signal reliability — worth cross-referencing with any other interviews touching on review monitoring

---

### Objection: No Defined Moat
- **Quote:** "Moat is very important. Moat is the thing which keeps your product stand long... I think you look for that."
- **Timestamp:** [37:43]
- **Severity:** High — long-term defensibility concern
- **Category:** Differentiation
- **Summary:** Soubhik pushed back on the absence of a clear defensible advantage. As AI lowers the cost of building competitive intelligence tools, a moat becomes essential. He did not see one articulated in the conversation and explicitly flagged it as a strategic gap.
- **Connects to:** New — moat/defensibility angle not typically surfaced early in discovery interviews

---

### Objection: Undercutting Legacy Pricing Is a Bad Strategy
- **Quote:** "Don't bound yourself saying that okay, this competition is doing 40,000, we need to build something where we do less than that. Don't bound yourself with that. That's a very bad strategy."
- **Timestamp:** [23:14] – [24:03]
- **Severity:** Medium — challenges the current pricing strategy of Kompete.ai (target: fraction of Klue/Crayon)
- **Category:** Pricing
- **Summary:** Soubhik warns that pricing below competitors as a market entry strategy leads to thin margins and undermines perceived value. He recommends pricing on demonstrated ROI rather than relative to competition — even suggesting Kompete.ai could justify charging *more* than Klue if the value can be quantified.
- **Connects to:** Pricing strategy — directly counter to Kompete.ai's stated $2–5K/yr positioning

## Strategic Insights

### Insight: B2B Product Roadmaps Are Customer-Dictated, Not Competition-Driven
- **Quote:** "In B2B mostly the biggest paying customer will dictate a lot of product roadmap for you. I have built many things which were told to us by our CSMs which came in from the customers."
- **Implication for Kompete:** The product team persona is a weak ICP because their roadmap inputs come from enterprise customers, not competitor monitoring. Marketing, sales, and CSM teams — who directly win/lose revenue based on competitive positioning — are the natural buyers.

---

### Insight: B2B Markets Consolidate to 1-2 Players — Monitoring is Only Critical in That Duopoly
- **Quote:** "Every market will consolidate to one or two players. That's how the Pareto works... if there are only two players in the market, then people do monitor each other. So you are very closely..."
- **Implication for Kompete:** The strongest pain is in markets with 2-5 well-defined competitors where every feature and pricing move matters. Kompete.ai should target companies in maturing, consolidating markets rather than fragmented early-stage ones.

---

### Insight: AI-Enabled Speed of Competitive Disruption Is a Tailwind for Kompete
- **Quote:** "Today in the world of AI and Claude has just recently released the co-worker agents also. Right. So that shipping speed and as well as the speed of disruption and new startups coming up will be at an all-time high for sure."
- **Implication for Kompete:** The AI disruption wave makes competitive monitoring more urgent, not less. This is a strong market timing argument — ship speed is increasing, so the window for late discovery of competitive moves is shrinking.

---

### Insight: People Are Already Doing Manual Competitive Intelligence — Just Very Inefficiently
- **Quote:** "I took free tier of all these companies, whichever I could take. I logged in not with the demand base account, with my personal account and I took pictures of all their screens and kept it. That's how... a one time thing which I could do."
- **Implication for Kompete:** The job-to-be-done is already occurring — just manually, inconsistently, and without updating. Kompete.ai's framing should be "automate the work you're already doing badly" rather than "here's a new habit to form."

---

### Insight: Pricing Strategy Should Be ROI-Based, Not Competitor-Relative
- **Quote:** "If the customer is willing to pay you 100, for sure, for sure — that does not value your product if you give it back to them. Customer values your product if you can earn dollar values for them."
- **Implication for Kompete:** Kompete.ai's current positioning as a "fraction of Klue/Crayon" pricing may actually signal low value. Reframing around ROI delivered (deals won, churn prevented, time saved) and pricing accordingly — potentially higher than legacy tools — may be a stronger go-to-market.

## Persona Signals

- **Buyer or User?** Primarily a **User** (PM) — not a budget owner for competitive intelligence tools. He explicitly redirected to sales/marketing as the buyer persona.
- **Willingness to pay:** Low for himself as a PM; but strongly implied **high willingness** for sales-facing use cases where ROI is demonstrable. Warned against low pricing signaling low value.
- **Decision process:** ROI-first — would want an analytics dashboard showing revenue attribution before committing. Prefers frameworks (Porter's Five Forces) and moat analysis over real-time monitoring. Would likely require a champion in sales/revenue ops to drive the purchase.
- **Current tools mentioned:**
  - G2, Capterra (B2B review monitoring — with skepticism)
  - Google Play Store (B2C reviews)
  - Vendr (crowdsourced B2B pricing intelligence)
  - Gainsight (CSM tool — suggested as integration/comparison point)
  - Klue / "Clue" (mentioned by Aditya as competitor)
  - Compete by Semrush (mentioned by Aditya as competitor)
  - ZoomInfo, 6sense (competitors of Demand Base)
  - Glean (used as messaging evolution case study)
