# Interview Extract: Mukesh Agrawal

**Date:** 11th February, 2026
**Role/Company:** Senior Product Manager, Ways.com (US #1 supercar app) — manages B2B SaaS products; previously built and launched a repair shop management SaaS platform
**Background:** 7+ years in product management. Recently built a B2B SaaS product (repair shop management) from scratch, during which he conducted hands-on competitive intelligence manually. Has a builder's POV on both using and evaluating competitive tools.

---

## Objections & Challenges Raised

### Objection: ICP Ambiguity — Platform Serves Too Many Masters
- **Quote:** "For me right now, my intent was the mapping between the user and the data sets. I think that part needs to be fine-tuned... otherwise it should not feel overwhelming and clutter to the consumer."
- **Timestamp:** [30:02] – [30:41]
- **Severity:** High — strikes directly at the GTM and positioning clarity of the product
- **Category:** Market Fit
- **Summary:** Mukesh identifies three distinct user personas who might use the platform — product builders, software buyers, and investors — each requiring a materially different data set. He challenges whether the platform has a clear enough primary ICP. Without tighter persona focus, the platform risks feeling like an undifferentiated data dump to any given user, reducing willingness to pay.
- **Connects to:** New — but thematically relates to buyer persona confusion seen in early-stage B2B SaaS products broadly

---

### Objection: Competitor Growth Rate Data is Unattainable
- **Quote:** "The first one [growth rate] is a little difficult to solve, I would say, at least from my lens."
- **Timestamp:** [16:45] – [16:50] *(Aditya's response, confirming Mukesh's implied concern)*
- **Severity:** Medium — limits the scope of "must-have" real-time tracking use cases the platform can serve
- **Category:** Product
- **Summary:** Mukesh's top-priority real-time signal is competitor growth rate (e.g., month-on-month new customer onboarding). This is a data point the platform currently cannot deliver, and Aditya himself acknowledges this limitation. While secondary signals (launch tracking, churn alerts) are addressable, the single most desired metric sits out of reach.
- **Connects to:** New — competitor revenue/growth data gap is a structural limitation of public-data-based CI tools

---

### Objection: No Beta Product — Can't Self-Validate Value
- **Quote:** "Is there any beta link that you can share? Like I can do my own research around the platform. Is there any link openly?"
- **Timestamp:** [35:49]
- **Severity:** Medium — signals that Mukesh's interest is genuine but trust must be earned through hands-on access
- **Category:** GTM
- **Summary:** Mukesh explicitly asked for a self-serve link to explore the product independently. The absence of one means evaluation and trust-building are entirely dependent on founder-led demos, which is a bottleneck for scaling early discovery. This limits organic interest from converting to commitment.
- **Connects to:** New — early-stage product completeness / GTM readiness

---

### Objection: Persona Sprawl Creates Product Complexity Risk
- **Quote:** "If you want to cater all these three users at a portion of — then how can we combine and show all of this three different data sets into one platform so that it can address the needs of all different use cases."
- **Timestamp:** [29:19] – [30:02]
- **Severity:** High — multi-persona product strategy without sequencing is a known startup failure mode
- **Category:** GTM
- **Summary:** Mukesh draws from his own experience building a repair shop SaaS, where he deliberately picked one sub-persona (full-service shops) before expanding. He suggests Kompete risks building a product that serves no one exceptionally well by trying to serve too many personas simultaneously. He recommends picking one anchor persona and designing the data layer around their specific needs first.
- **Connects to:** New — but resonates with classic "nail one niche before scaling" GTM discipline

---

### Objection: Value Prop Clarity — What Does a User Actually Get vs. What They Need?
- **Quote:** "Only X and Y for me. I won't be going for ABC and other data sets. Right. So how can you identify what user needs and show only X and Y things to them — and that's where it will be valuable and then I will come and pay."
- **Timestamp:** [32:20] – [32:43]
- **Severity:** High — directly tied to willingness to pay and product stickiness
- **Category:** Value Prop
- **Summary:** Mukesh frames value in terms of relevance: a user will pay if and only if the platform surfaces the exact signals they care about, without forcing them to wade through irrelevant data. He signals that a broadly comprehensive but poorly personalized platform won't convert — the "all data, one screen" approach may feel impressive in a demo but underwhelming in daily use.
- **Connects to:** ICP Ambiguity objection above; also connects to Information Overload pain point

## Pain Points Validated

### Pain: Manual, Multi-Source Competitive Research Process
- **Quote:** "I just took the free demo of these two platforms, I scheduled a demo call and their sales team helped me walk through the platform... then there are third-party platforms which have comparisons of different softwares... I used all of this open platforms to do the competitor analysis."
- **Severity:** HIGH
- **Category:** Manual Process / Tool Fragmentation
- **Currently solving with:** Demo calls with competitor sales teams, taking paid subscriptions to use products directly, third-party SaaS comparison sites (unnamed, possibly G2/Capterra-style), Tracxn for financials, G2 for reviews

---

### Pain: Bi-Weekly Manual Check-Ins for Competitor Monitoring
- **Quote:** "Mostly once in two weeks I will go and check, okay, if something has been launched or not. So how I'll do it — I will just interact with the existing customer of that particular company... second one is by checking the product on your own... third one is product release notes."
- **Severity:** HIGH
- **Category:** Manual Process / Stale Data
- **Currently solving with:** (1) Informal network of "shadow customers" using competitor products, (2) personal product subscriptions with manual logins, (3) reading competitor press releases and product release notes

---

### Pain: No Centralized System of Record for Competitive Intel
- **Quote:** "Today, the data or basically the information around competitors sits in different departments... the TA team will have idea around hiring, the marketing will have idea around messaging positioning... all these things are siloed today." *(Aditya describing the problem; Mukesh confirms and elaborates)*
- **Severity:** HIGH
- **Category:** No System of Record / Tool Fragmentation
- **Currently solving with:** Mental model + personal notes; no dedicated system observed

---

### Pain: Missing Signals From Unmonitored Channels
- **Quote:** "Say someone is saying something on Reddit — we are not able to constantly monitor unless we have someone or a team to kind of monitor that on a daily basis. But there will be definite aspects where we will drop the ball, right?"
- **Severity:** MEDIUM
- **Category:** Late Discovery / Manual Process
- **Currently solving with:** Not solving — acknowledged as a blind spot

---

### Pain: Competitor Churn Intel — No Visibility Into Who Is Leaving Competitors
- **Quote:** "Second one is the data on the customers who are churning from my competitor so that I can go and ask them — hey, you were using this product but I see that now you are discontinuing to use that. Can you use mine?"
- **Severity:** HIGH
- **Category:** Other (Competitive Sales Trigger)
- **Currently solving with:** Not solving systematically — relies on informal relationship network and word of mouth

---

### Pain: Information Overload Risk From Competitive Reports
- **Quote:** "Bi-weekly would help because there are so many things going on. So if I will get reports quickly it will be too overwhelming for me to go and check."
- **Severity:** MEDIUM
- **Category:** Information Overload
- **Currently solving with:** Cadence management — deliberately limiting frequency of competitive check-ins to avoid overwhelm

## Strategic Insights

### Insight: "Shadow Customer" Networks Are an Informal Competitive Intelligence Channel
- **Quote:** "So how I'll do it — I will just interact with the existing customer of that particular company. Hey, did you find any change? Have they rolled out anything new? Like do you want to try out my product? So creating a network with those shadow customers."
- **Implication for Kompete:** Product builders at SMB/early-stage SaaS are already doing informal CI through customer networks — Kompete can position as the systematic, always-on version of this relationship-based intelligence gathering. This is a compelling "replace the workaround" narrative for GTM messaging.

---

### Insight: Investors Are an Organic, Unprompted Use Case
- **Quote:** "This is also valuable for firms who are looking to invest in a company in certain domain. They can come, they can have a competitive benchmark analysis."
- **Implication for Kompete:** Mukesh independently surfaced investors as a use case without prompting. This suggests the product's data layer has cross-persona appeal. VC/PE firms doing sector diligence could be a high-willingness-to-pay adjacent segment worth validating separately.

---

### Insight: Product Builders vs. Software Buyers Have Fundamentally Different CI Needs
- **Quote:** "There are two sets of people — one who are building the platform, one who are purchasing the platform. For [the purchasing persona] pricing becomes the core part."
- **Implication for Kompete:** This is a critical segmentation insight. Kompete's current positioning (pricing audit → strategic CI) may primarily resonate with builders, not buyers. If targeting buyers, the product needs a different emphasis — comparison clarity over predictive strategy.

---

### Insight: Competitor Release Notes Are an Underutilized Free Signal
- **Quote:** "Company generally publish product release notes or press releases. So with that, you know — okay, this is something that they are trying or building."
- **Implication for Kompete:** Mukesh monitors release notes manually as part of his bi-weekly cadence. Automating release note ingestion and summarization into the Kompete platform would directly replace a known manual workflow — a clear "time savings" value prop for product personas.

---

## Persona Signals

- **Buyer or User?** Both — Mukesh is a product manager who would be a primary *user* of the platform (he did the CI work himself), but at Way.com he also appears to hold budget influence for early-stage tools given the company's flat structure ("ownership is high in terms of sales, growth and marketing")
- **Willingness to pay:** Moderate-to-high, but conditional on persona-matched relevance. His exact framing: "when it's giving me a value prop, when I'm looking for something and it addresses that — that's when I'll purchase it." Would not pay for a data-rich but unfocused product.
- **Decision process:** Self-serve trial first ("Is there any beta link I can explore?"), followed by evaluation of whether insights are relevant to his specific needs. Would not buy purely on a founder demo — needs hands-on validation. Likely a bottom-up buyer.
- **Current tools mentioned:** Tracxn (company financials/intelligence), G2 (customer reviews), Capterra (implied), unnamed SaaS comparison platforms, competitor product subscriptions (direct), competitor demo calls, informal customer network ("shadow customers"), product release notes
