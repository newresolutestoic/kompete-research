**Root question:** Is fixing internal process a higher-ROI investment than competitive intelligence?

## Dimension 2.2: CI as Process Input
> Does embedding CI into process work? And does it actually get adopted?

| When CI is embedded | Impact | Source |
|--------------------|--------|--------|
| Companies using battlecards → improved win rates | 71% | [src-battlecard](../sources/src-battlecard-effectiveness.md) |
| Of those, improvement exceeds 20% | 93% | [src-battlecard](../sources/src-battlecard-effectiveness.md) |
| Daily CI engagement with sales | 84% effectiveness increase | [src-crayon](../sources/src-crayon-state-of-ci-2025.md) |

| When CI is bolted on | Impact | Source |
|---------------------|--------|--------|
| Reps who actually use battlecards | Only 26% | [src-battlecard](../sources/src-battlecard-effectiveness.md) |
| Compete teams integrated into sales tools | Less than 50% | [src-crayon](../sources/src-crayon-state-of-ci-2025.md) |

**KEY INSIGHT:** "Process vs CI" is an objection to CI's FORM FACTOR, not its value.
The objection tells you HOW to build (embedded, not dashboard), not WHETHER to build.

**Dimension verdict:** CI embedded in process wins. CI as a separate system loses.

## OBJECTION 2 OVERALL VERDICT

**Process matters — genuinely. But CI is a process INPUT, not a competing priority.**
The objection is really about CI's form factor: don't build a dashboard nobody checks.
Build CI that's invisible — embedded in CRM, surfaced in deal flow, zero behavioral change.
