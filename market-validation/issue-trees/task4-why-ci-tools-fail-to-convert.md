# Issue Tree: Why CI Tools Fail to Convert

**Root question:** If CI pain is validated, why don't prospects buy?

---

## The Compound Conversion Barrier

It's not one problem. It's 7 interlocking barriers that multiply against each other.

```
100 prospects who feel CI pain
 │
 ├── Dim 1: "Nice to Have" filter → 50 remain
 │   └── CI is a vitamin, not a painkiller. Phase 3 of tech stack buying.
 │       15/27 interviewees called it "good to have"
 │
 ├── Dim 2: Attribution filter → 30 remain
 │   └── Can't tie CI to revenue. <50% of compete teams have KPIs.
 │       $40K CI tool loses to $40K outreach tool with clear metrics.
 │
 ├── Dim 3: Urgency filter → 15 remain
 │   └── No forcing function. No compliance, no audit, no deadline.
 │       CI triggers are intermittent (lost deal, board prep).
 │
 ├── Dim 4: Buyer journey filter → 8 remain
 │   └── Pain holder (rep) ≠ solution owner (PMM) ≠ budget holder (CMO).
 │       52% of programs have no exec sponsor.
 │
 ├── Dim 5: DIY filter → 5 remain
 │   └── ChatGPT + spreadsheets + intern = "good enough" at 1/10th cost.
 │       47% of CI leaders already use free ChatGPT.
 │
 ├── Dim 6: Adoption filter → 3 remain
 │   └── 26% of reps use battlecards. 70% of rollouts fail on ROI.
 │       Behavioral change kills what procurement approved.
 │
 └── Dim 7: Category filter → 2 remain
     └── "CI tool" isn't how buyers articulate the problem.
         82% haven't formalized CI process. Category may not be standalone.

Result: ~2% of validated pain converts to long-term customers.
```

## Dimension 1: The "Nice to Have" Trap

| Evidence | Data | Source |
|---------|------|--------|
| SaaS unicorns: painkillers vs vitamins | 80% painkillers, <20% vitamins | Knight Capital framework |
| Conversion rate gap | Painkillers ~37%, vitamins ~4% | Knight Capital |
| Tech stack buying order | CI = Phase 3 (after CRM, automation, analytics) | LaGrowthMachine, Webstacks |
| Interview validation | 15/27 called CI "good to have" for B2B | Kompete interviews |
| Product Manager importance rating | 7.5/10 — interested, not buying | Kompete persona synthesis |

**Verdict:** CI is structurally a vitamin. The "should I focus on customers" objection is actually the vitamin problem in disguise.

## Dimension 2: The Attribution Problem

| Evidence | Data | Source |
|---------|------|--------|
| Compete teams with KPIs | Less than 50% | Crayon 2025 |
| Best CI metrics | Correlational, not causal | CIA, Crayon |
| Intangible value problem | "Confidence when competitor's name is mentioned" — try putting that in CFO spreadsheet | Practitioner consensus |
| Budget competition | $40K CI tool vs $40K outreach tool with clear pipeline metrics | Structural analysis |

**Verdict:** When the CFO asks "what's the ROI?" and the answer is "it's hard to measure," the PO gets deprioritized. CI's attribution gap makes procurement structurally harder than tools with clear metrics.

---

## Dimension 3: The Urgency Problem

| Evidence | Data | Source |
|---------|------|--------|
| CI triggers are intermittent | Lost deal, competitor launch, board prep — not daily | RevenueHero, Salesmotion |
| No external forcing function | No regulation, no compliance, no audit | Structural analysis |
| CI is sporadic | 13/27 interviewees: not continuous work | Kompete interviews |
| Comparison: security tools | Get bought after breach or regulatory mandate — CI has no equivalent | DemandMaven |

**Verdict:** CI is "important but not urgent" — the deadliest category in SaaS. No forcing function pushes the purchase past evaluation.

---

## Dimension 4: The Buyer Journey Problem

| Role | Feels pain | Owns solution | Controls budget | Result |
|------|-----------|---------------|----------------|--------|
| Sales reps | HIGH | No | No | Won't advocate |
| PMMs | Medium | YES | No | Can't sign checks |
| VP Sales/CRO | Low | No | YES | Not motivated enough |
| CMO | Low | No | YES | 20+ priorities ahead of CI |
| CEO ($5-50M) | HIGH | Partial | YES | **Only buyer where pain + budget align** |

- 52% of compete programs have no sales exec sponsor
- PMM is an "orphan function" — budget shifts between CPO, CEO, CBO
- "Influence without authority" is the PMM's structural problem

**Verdict:** The only buyer where pain and budget authority align is the CEO/founder at $5-50M ARR. Every other buyer path is structurally broken.

- Klue CEO confirmed: "losing deals to ChatGPT"

## Dimension 6: The Behavioral Change Problem

| Evidence | Data | Source |
|---------|------|--------|
| Reps using battlecards | 26% | Klue |
| Reps accessing CI before Stage 2 | 31% | Forrester 2025 |
| Software rollouts failing on ROI | 70% | Gartner |
| Enterprise features actually used | 49% | Pendo 2023 |
| Wasted spend per employee on unused software | $10,000+/year | Zylo 2024 |

**Why battlecards fail specifically:**
- Too long, too vague, built for marketing not sales
- Only 43% include talk tracks; only 19% provide evidence
- Go stale within a week
- Not inside the CRM where reps already live

**Verdict:** Even when procurement succeeds, behavioral change kills adoption. The tool must require ZERO behavioral change — it must show up where people already work, not require them to go somewhere new.

## Dimension 7: The Category Creation Problem

| Evidence | Data | Source |
|---------|------|--------|
| CI leaders with mature program | Only 18% | Klue maturity model |
| Not satisfied with CI maturity | 86% | Klue maturity model |
| Klue CEO post-layoffs | "Ambiguity about what people will pay for" | BetaKit |
| Klue needs to | "Re-find product-market fit" | StartupEcosystem |
| Buyers searching for "CI tool" | Extremely low search volume | Category analysis |

**Verdict:** 82% of prospects haven't formalized the process CI tools automate. You can't sell an advanced tool to a market that hasn't reached the maturity to use it. And "competitive intelligence" isn't the language buyers use to describe their problem — they say "I keep losing deals" or "our battlecards are stale."

## WHAT THIS MEANS FOR KOMPETE

The 7 barriers are not independent. They multiply. That's why the category has a ~2% conversion rate from validated pain to long-term customer.

**To break through, you must defeat EACH barrier independently:**

| Barrier | How to defeat it |
|---------|-----------------|
| 1. Vitamin → Painkiller | Attach to acute trigger: "you lost this deal because..." not "track competitors generally" |
| 2. Attribution | Build win-rate tracking INTO the product. Show before/after. Make the ROI case automatic. |
| 3. Urgency | Create urgency via alerts: "Competitor just dropped pricing, 3 of your active deals affected." Time-sensitive = urgent. |
| 4. Buyer journey | Sell to CEO/founder at $5-50M (pain + budget in one person). Skip PMM entirely. |
| 5. DIY | Price below DIY ($200-500/mo, not $3K+/mo). OR deliver value DIY structurally cannot (continuous monitoring, system of record). |
| 6. Behavioral change | Zero behavioral change. Push to Slack/email/CRM. Never require login to a dashboard. |
| 7. Category | Don't say "competitive intelligence." Say "win more deals" or "competitive revenue protection." |

**The meta-insight:** Every one of these solutions points in the same direction → **push-based, embedded, AI-native, priced low, sold to the person who feels the pain AND holds the budget.** This is a fundamentally different product from Klue/Crayon.
