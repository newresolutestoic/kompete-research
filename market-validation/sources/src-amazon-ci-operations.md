## Why this source matters
Amazon is the most-cited example of "customer obsession over competitor obsession." This source proves the gap between what Amazon SAYS and what Amazon DOES — the definitive case for the false dichotomy.

## Key data points
- Amazon Leadership Principle: "Although leaders pay attention to competitors, they obsess over customers"
- **Reality:** Amazon had automated crawlers slowly and randomly crawling every page of Zappos' website
- Amazon purchased multiple SKUs from Zappos delivered to Amazon Fulfillment Centers to benchmark fulfillment quality and test returns process
- Amazon ran a **"Global Intelligence Program" (GIP)** with dedicated intelligence analysts
- GIP job postings grew from **7 in 2018 to 46 by 2020**
- As of 2026, LinkedIn shows **310+ Amazon competitive intelligence jobs** in the US

## The Bezos paradox explained
- Amazon is customer-obsessed as a **PRIORITY FRAMEWORK** (where to focus strategic decisions)
- Amazon is competitor-aware as an **OPERATIONAL REALITY** (how to execute better)
- The slogan is aspirational positioning, not operational guidance
- Founders who cite Bezos are copying the marketing, not the operations
