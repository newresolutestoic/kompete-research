## Key data points

### Effectiveness
- **71% of businesses** using battlecards report improved win rates (Klue)
- Among those with improved win rates, **93% say improvement exceeds 20%**
- Financial services case study: real-time battle cards increased **average deal size by 25%** and **competitive win rate by 30%**
- Pharma case study: targeted battle cards → **32% increase in conversion rates** and **23% boost in customer retention** over two years
- **Battle cards updated monthly → up to 59% win rate lift** (Crayon)

### The adoption gap (critical)
- **Only 26% of reps** actually use the battlecards their product marketing team creates
- **50%+ of compete teams** don't integrate into sales' existing platforms/tools (Crayon 2025)
- Intelligence sits in a separate system reps never open

### Win-loss program integration
- **67%** of win-loss programs managed alongside competitive intelligence
- Integrated CI/win-loss programs are **2x more likely to report transformational impact** (12%) vs non-integrated (2%)
- **94%** of organizations maintaining or increasing win-loss budget
- **98%** of win-loss programs have executive visibility

### Best practices for high adoption
1. Battle cards integrated into CRM/sales workflow (not separate PDFs)
2. Role-playing exercises based on actual win/loss data
3. Continuous feedback loops from field reps back to CI team
4. Daily or weekly CI engagement with sales (not quarterly)
5. Conversational intelligence (Gong, Chorus) monitoring competitor mentions in real-time

## Direct implication for Kompete
- Battle cards work (71% improved win rates) but adoption is the bottleneck (26%)
- The form factor matters more than the content quality
- Kompete already has battle cards built — the question is delivery mechanism, not generation
