## Key data points by dimension

### Vitamin vs Painkiller
- 80% of SaaS unicorns are painkillers; <20% are vitamins
- Painkiller apps: ~37% store conversion. Vitamins: ~4%
- CI falls in Phase 3 of tech stack buying (after CRM, automation, analytics)
- 15/27 Kompete interviewees called CI "good to have" for B2B

### Attribution
- Less than half of compete teams have KPIs in place (Crayon 2025)
- CI champion cannot produce clear attribution chain → deprioritized by CFO
- $40K CI tool competes against $40K outreach tool with clear pipeline-per-dollar metrics

### Urgency
- CI triggers are intermittent and non-critical (lost deal, competitor launch, board prep)
- No regulatory forcing function, no compliance deadline, no audit requirement
- 13/27 interviewees: CI is sporadic, not continuous

### Buyer journey
- Sales reps (feel pain) → zero budget authority
- PMMs (own workflow) → limited purchasing power, "influence without authority"
- VP Sales/CMO (control budget) → 20+ competing priorities
- 52% of compete programs have no sales exec sponsor

### DIY + ChatGPT
- Klue CEO: "losing deals to ChatGPT"
- DIY stack: $2-4K/month. Klue: $3-7K/month.
- CI leaders using: free ChatGPT (47%), paid ChatGPT (45%), Gemini (38%)
- 16/27 Kompete interviewees already use ChatGPT for CI

### Behavioral change
- 26% of reps use battlecards
- 31% access competitive content before deal Stage 2 (Forrester)
- 70% of software rollouts fail to deliver expected ROI (Gartner)
- Only 43% of battlecards include talk tracks; only 19% provide evidence

### Category
- 18% of CI leaders feel they have a mature program
- 82% haven't formalized the process CI tools automate
- Klue CEO: "ambiguity about what people will pay for" + needs to "re-find product-market fit"
