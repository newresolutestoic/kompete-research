## Why this source matters
Clozd's unique contribution: they compare what CRMs say about lost deals vs what buyers actually say when interviewed. This reveals the gap between what companies THINK they know and what's actually happening.

## Key data points
- CRM competitor data is **incorrect in approximately 70% of deals** (based on 1,000+ closed-lost deals vs buyer interviews)
- Buyer and seller reasons for lost deals **align only 15% of the time** — 85% mismatch
- **44% of closed-lost reasons in CRM** are "outcomes, not reasons" (lack actionable insight)
- Average buyer shares **4-5 primary decision factors**; CRM captures a single dropdown
- **84% of companies** doing win-loss analysis for 2+ years report increased win rates
- **85%** of ongoing, cross-functional programs see positive ROI vs. only **55%** for project-based ones

## Direct implications
- Companies ARE tracking competitors — they're just doing it wrong (70% error rate)
- The "process is fine" objection is undermined: your process is built on wrong data
- Win-loss is the bridge between CI and process — exactly where Kompete should position
