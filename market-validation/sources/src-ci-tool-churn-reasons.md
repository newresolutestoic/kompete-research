## Root causes of CI churn (synthesized across sources)

1. **Adoption failure** — Sales reps don't use battlecards in workflow. Tool becomes PMM island.
2. **Signal-to-noise** — Too much data, not enough insight. "Reviewing tons of junk."
3. **ROI attribution** — Can't directly tie CI to revenue. Renewal justification fails during budget cuts.
4. **DIY competition** — Companies building own CI with ChatGPT/Claude + web scraping.
5. **High-touch onboarding** — 3+ weeks setup, requires dedicated CI champion.
6. **Price sensitivity** — $20-40K/year hard to justify when adoption is low.

## STRUCTURAL insight
The buyer-user mismatch is the core churn driver:
- **Buyer:** Product Marketing (buys it, maintains it)
- **User:** Sales reps (supposed to use battlecards, mostly don't — 26% adoption)
- Result: PMM can't prove ROI → budget cut → cancel

This is the classic "admin tool" death spiral. The person paying is not the person using.
