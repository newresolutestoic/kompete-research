## Threat assessment by platform

### Gong — MODERATE-HIGH threat
- Smart Trackers: keyword monitoring for competitor mentions across all calls
- "Ask Anything": natural language queries across all conversations/deals
- "Deep Researcher" agent for deal preparation
- 70% faster processing
- Positions as "Revenue AI OS"
- DOES: capture competitor mentions at source (calls)
- DOES NOT: battlecard creation, external monitoring (websites, reviews, news), competitive signal aggregation, historical competitor tracking

### Clari + Salesloft — HIGH threat (most dangerous adjacency)
- Dec 2025 merger: ~$450M combined ARR
- Chorus (conversation intel) + pipeline analytics + CI features
- "Trend Analysis Agent" automatically uncovers competitive activity patterns
- AEs get "AI-generated competitive intelligence, stakeholder mapping"
- MASSIVE distribution advantage
- Actively building CI as a feature within deal intelligence workflow

### HubSpot — LOW threat
- Lightweight: competitor battlecard templates via custom properties
- Workflow-based competitor tagging in deals
- Smart Data Capture identifies competitors in calls
- New AEO tool with competitor Share of Voice
- Buyer Intent: company news signals
- "Good enough" for SMBs who won't pay $20-40K/year for Klue
- NOT a serious CI platform

### Salesforce — NO threat (currently)
- Einstein focuses on lead scoring, forecasting, activity capture
- NO dedicated CI module in Sales Cloud
- No evidence of CI-specific product development

## Net assessment
The "surrounded" problem is real. Gong from conversation intel, Clari from revenue intel, HubSpot from CRM are all adding CI-like features. NONE yet match dedicated CI for depth. But the gap is narrowing for "good enough" use cases.

Key risk: Clari+Salesloft at $450M ARR could build "good enough" CI that makes standalone tools unnecessary for most companies.
