## Bear signals (organized)

### 1. Klue growth stall
- $81M raised, Tiger Global backed — burned $28M to grow $2.5M
- 40% layoffs. CEO says "ambiguity about what people will pay for"
- Losing deals to ChatGPT
- Revenue: $25-50M CAD estimated (after years of investment)

### 2. Market is genuinely small
- Standalone CI tools: $0.6-0.9B total (Mordor, Fortune BI)
- Even at 20% CAGR → only $1.5B by 2030
- Limited exit outcomes unless expanding into adjacent markets

### 3. Platform bundling risk
- Gong: unified license at $250/user/month, includes basic competitive tracking (Smart Trackers)
- Revenue intelligence platforms: $60-150/user/month with some CI features
- Gartner renamed "CI Tools" to "CI Platforms" (2025) — signals integration trend

### 4. ChatGPT as real substitute
- Klue's own CEO confirmed losing deals to it
- Manual CI workflow (monitor, summarize, battlecard) is precisely what LLMs handle
- BUT: only handles 40% of workflow (see src-chatgpt-ci-limitations.md)

### 5. Adoption challenges
- Crayon "requires highly manual daily curation"
- Klue AI "is relatively young, insights not real-time, considerable noise"
- Average CI program takes 6-9 months to show statistically significant win rate improvement
- Only 26% of reps use battlecards even when they exist

### 6. No Tier-1 VC funding since 2021
- Last major CI round: Klue Series B (Dec 2021) — 4.5 year funding drought
- No a16z, Sequoia, Benchmark bet on standalone CI
- VC skepticism about "GPT wrappers"

## Counter-arguments (for balance)
- Klue stall may be company-level failure, not category-level (poor execution, wrong architecture)
- Small market today ≠ small market if reimagined as AI service (Sequoia's services thesis)
- Platform bundling adds basic CI, not deep CI — similar to how Salesforce reports don't replace BI tools
- ChatGPT substitution is for basic tasks; the 60% it can't do is where dedicated tools live
