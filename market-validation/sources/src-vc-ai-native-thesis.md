## a16z: "AI Will Eat Application Software" (2025/2026)
- AI does not kill software but changes WHICH companies win
- New entrants adopt superior business models incumbents can't replicate
- Example: Decagon replaces Zendesk with per-conversation pricing; incumbents can't match without cannibalizing revenue
- "System of record will start to lose primacy" as AI collapses intent → execution distance
- Seven enduring moats: network effects, process power, proprietary data, brand, scale, counter-positioning, cornered resource

## Sequoia: "Services Are the New Software" (2026)
- Julien Bek: "For every dollar spent on software, six are spent on services. AI can now do the work, not just sell the tool."
- Real opportunity = capturing LABOR budget, not software budget
- Sonya Huang (Sequoia, Gong board member) betting on AI in revenue intelligence
- Vertical agents create defensible value through domain-specific training data

## Bain: Agentic AI SaaS Disruption Framework (2025)
- Four quadrants: Core Strongholds, Open Doors, Gold Mines, Battlegrounds
- CI workflows = **Battleground** (high automation potential + high external AI penetration)
- Most contested and most disruptable quadrant

## Moonshot Capital: AI-Native vs AI-Enabled (2026)
- **AI-Enabled:** "Remove the intelligence layer and the company still functions." Software multiples.
- **AI-Native:** "Intelligence is structural. Removing AI eliminates business function." Different capital structure.
- Current Klue/Crayon = AI-enabled (added AI to existing workflows)
- Opportunity = AI-native (where AI IS the competitive analyst)
- "The era where 'we use AI' carried weight is finished" — VCs demand structural defensibility

## Implications for Kompete
- Must be AI-native (the AI IS the analyst), not AI-enabled (AI added to a dashboard)
- Price on outcomes (win rate lift), not seats
- Capture the labor budget ($400K+/year for a 50-person sales org doing manual CI)
- Build in the Battleground quadrant — most disruptable but most contested
