# Source: Management Science Field Experiment — Kim, INSEAD (2025)

## Metadata
- **Title:** "The Value of Competitor Information: Evidence from a Field Experiment"
- **Authors:** Hyunjin Kim (INSEAD)
- **Publication:** Management Science, Vol. 71(4), April 2025
- **URL:** https://pubsonline.informs.org/doi/10.1287/mnsc.2022.04062
- **Date accessed:** 2026-05-04
- **Type:** Peer-reviewed academic paper, randomized controlled trial

## Why this source matters
This is the ONLY causal evidence (not correlational) that competitor information drives revenue. Most CI studies are surveys or case studies — this is a proper RCT across 3,218 firms.

## Key data points
- **Study design:** Randomly assigned firms to receive competitor pricing information vs. control group
- **Sample:** 3,218 firms
- **Result:** Treatment firms saw **4.8% higher revenues**, **8% more employees/customers** at endline, **3% lower availability** (indicating more business)
- **Mechanism:** Nearly half of firms at baseline **lacked knowledge of competitor prices**. Performance gains were driven mainly by **firms that were overpricing** — they didn't know because they weren't looking.

## Direct implications
- Proves that competitor information has a causal (not just correlational) effect on revenue
- The firms that benefited most were those who had NO competitive awareness before — exactly Kompete's ICP
- "Overpricing without knowing" = the exact scenario where CI saves deals
